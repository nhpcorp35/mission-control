# Mission Control Principles

Mission Control exists to make AI-assisted software development safer, faster, and more reproducible.

It is infrastructure, not the product.

Every design decision should be evaluated against these principles.

---

## 1. Product First

Mission Control exists to accelerate product development.

Today, that product is Legal AI.

Mission Control must never become a distraction from delivering attorney value, validating with real matters, or building a successful business.

---

## 2. Simplicity Over Complexity

The simplest solution that accomplishes the mission is preferred.

Avoid unnecessary abstraction, frameworks, and infrastructure.

Every new feature must justify its existence.

---

## 3. Safety Before Speed

Repository safety is more important than automation.

Every engineering action should be reversible, reviewable, and auditable.

The system must make it difficult to accidentally damage a project.

---

## 4. Human Decision Authority

Allen defines priorities.

Hal provides technical leadership.

Coding agents execute engineering work.

Mission Control enforces approved boundaries.

Final engineering decisions always remain with the human.

---

## 5. Agent Independence

Mission Control must not depend on any specific coding agent.

Cursor, Codex, Claude, and future agents should all be replaceable without changing the architecture.

---

## 6. Mission Driven

Every engineering activity begins with a mission.

A mission defines:

- objective
- scope
- permissions
- deliverables
- approval requirements

The agent executes the mission.

The mission defines the work.

---

## 7. Reproducibility

Every mission should be repeatable.

Results should be reproducible.

Engineering decisions should be documented.

Nothing important should exist only inside a chat.

---

## 8. Continuous Improvement

Mission Control should evolve only when real development work reveals unnecessary friction.

Infrastructure follows proven need.

Never speculative engineering.

---

## 9. Earn Generalization

Mission Control is being proven through Legal AI.

General-purpose capability is earned through repeated success—not assumed from the beginning.

Every abstraction should first solve a real problem.

---

## 10. Build Trust

The system succeeds only if developers trust it.

Trust is built through:

- transparency
- predictable behavior
- review before irreversible actions
- reliable execution
- clear responsibility
